The 360° Skyline Song

Katsufumi Matsui

Technical cooperation Kazunori Ogasawara Seico Okamoto

Special thanks to Seiichiro Matsumura Boyoung Kim Tatsuya Ogusu Toru Nakamoto Kohei Morita

‘The 360° Skyline Song’ is an audio-visual installation work that makes sound waves from the visual “boundary” between surrounding scenery and sky. The visual data, made by recording the surrounding scenery with a video camera rotating an angle of 360 degrees, are transformed into sound waves in real time by analyzing each camera frame. By acquiring the color temperature and the atmospheric temperature there simultaneously, ‘The 360° Skyline Song’ improvises the expressive synthesis unique to the moment, so they are live and impressive to the audience. Both audio and visual data achieve the intuitive recognition of the transition of the boundary in sceneries. This installation implies the instability of surroundings, by interactively producing sound from changing surroundings and showing the captured movie.

Boundary Synthesizer I

Katsufumi Matsui

Technical cooperation Seico Okamoto

Boundary Synthesizer I is an interactive audio-visual installation work that makes sound waves by analysis of the visual “boundary” of sceneries.Those sceneries come from various videos. The computer vision system detects the boundary lines of sceneries, such as cityscapes, sea waves and fireworks, automatically. This boundary line is extracted from the outline in each video frame and is directly transformed into the sound wave line. Users can manipulate parameters by turning knobs and pushing switches of the interface as with typical musical synthesizers. Thus, this installation is an audio-visual synthesizer in which the oscillator’s waveform is structured by the visual boundary. This synthesizer has 2 oscillators – 2 video images are encoded simultaneously. Users can enjoy playing with Boundary Synthesizer I by changing video inputs, controlling the frequency of ring modulation and pressing switches that modulate both video and sound. Monotonous sounds are made from monotonous scenery; complex sounds are made from scenery with active movements, for example sea waves and fireworks. Users can experience the intuitive connection of scenery and sound.
